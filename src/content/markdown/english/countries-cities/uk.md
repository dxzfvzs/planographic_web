# The United Kingdom

The **United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland (the UK)** is an island country in north-west Europe. It is famous for its history, its monarchy, its language – and its rainy weather.

---

## Parts of the UK

The UK is made of **four countries**:

- **England** – the largest part; capital **London** ([[countries-cities/london]]),
- **Scotland** – capital Edinburgh; mountains (the Highlands), lakes (Loch Ness), kilts and bagpipes,
- **Wales** – capital Cardiff; Welsh is spoken there as well as English,
- **Northern Ireland** – capital Belfast; it shares the island with the Republic of Ireland.

**Great Britain** = England + Scotland + Wales (the island). The UK also includes Northern Ireland.

## Basic facts

- Population: about **67 million**; currency: **pound sterling (£)**.
- The flag is called the **Union Jack** – it combines the crosses of England, Scotland and Ireland.
- The UK **left the European Union in 2020 (Brexit)**.
- Geography: no place is far from the sea; the longest river is the **Severn**, the highest mountain **Ben Nevis** (Scotland). The weather is mild and changeable – people talk about it all the time.

## Political system

- The UK is a **constitutional monarchy** – the head of state is the **King (Charles III)**, but his role is mostly ceremonial.
- The country is governed by the **Parliament** (House of Commons and House of Lords) and the **Prime Minister**, who lives at 10 Downing Street.

## Famous for…

- **The monarchy** and royal family, Buckingham Palace, changing of the guard.
- Writers: **William Shakespeare**, Jane Austen, Charles Dickens, J. K. Rowling ([[culture-leisure/literature]]).
- Music: The Beatles, The Rolling Stones, Queen, Ed Sheeran, Adele.
- **Football** (Premier League), rugby, cricket, tennis (Wimbledon).
- Traditions: **afternoon tea**, pubs, fish and chips ([[culture-leisure/food-meals]]), driving on the left, red double-decker buses and telephone boxes.
