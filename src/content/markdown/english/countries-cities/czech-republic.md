# The Czech Republic

The Czech Republic (also called **Czechia**) is my home country. It is a small country **in the heart of Europe** – it
has no sea, but it has beautiful nature, historic towns and a rich culture.

---

## Basic facts

- **Location:** Central Europe; neighbours are **Germany, Poland, Slovakia and Austria**.
- **Population:** about **10.9 million** people.
- **Capital city:** **Prague** ([[countries-cities/prague]]).
- **Language:** Czech; **currency:** Czech crown (koruna).
- Member of the **EU** and **NATO**; until 1993 it was part of **Czechoslovakia**, then it peacefully split into the
  Czech Republic and Slovakia.

## Geography

- Three historical parts: **Bohemia** (west), **Moravia** (east) and **Silesia** (north-east).
- The landscape in Bohemia is quite hilly, but in Moravia, lots of flat fields take over. The highest mountain is *
  *Sněžka (1,603 m)** in the Krkonoše mountains.
- Important rivers: the **Vltava** (the longest) and the **Labe (Elbe)**, which flows to the North Sea.
- The weather is mild – we have all **four seasons**.

## Cities and places to visit

- **Prague** – the capital, famous for its castle and old town.
- **Brno** – the second biggest city, the centre of Moravia; I study here.
- **Ostrava** (industry), **Plzeň** (beer), **Olomouc**, **Český Krumlov** (a UNESCO town).
- Many **castles and chateaux** (Karlštejn, Lednice), spa towns (**Karlovy Vary**) and national parks (Šumava, Czech
  Switzerland).

## What is the Czech Republic famous for?

- **Beer** – Pilsner Urquell and Budvar; Czechs drink the most beer per person in the world.
- **Ice hockey and football**; famous athletes like Jaromír Jágr or Ester Ledecká.
- Music and film: composers **Dvořák and Smetana**, director Miloš Forman.
- Inventions and products: contact lenses (Otto Wichterle), Škoda cars, Czech glass.
- Writers like **Karel Čapek** (he invented the word "robot") and Franz Kafka.
