# London

London is the **capital of England and the whole UK** ([[countries-cities/uk]]). With about **9 million people** it is one of the biggest and most multicultural cities in Europe – you can hear hundreds of languages there.

---

## Basic facts

- It lies on the **river Thames** in south-east England.
- It is a world centre of **finance, culture and tourism**.
- The city was founded by the **Romans** (Londinium); in 1666 a large part of it burned down in the **Great Fire of London**.

## Main sights

- **The Houses of Parliament** with the famous clock tower – people call the bell inside **Big Ben**.
- **Buckingham Palace** – the London home of the King; tourists watch the **changing of the guard**.
- **The Tower of London** – an old fortress where the **Crown Jewels** are kept; guarded by "Beefeaters" and the famous ravens; next to it stands **Tower Bridge**.
- **Westminster Abbey** – the church where kings and queens are crowned.
- **St Paul's Cathedral**, **Trafalgar Square** with Nelson's Column, **Piccadilly Circus**.
- **The London Eye** – a giant observation wheel with a great view of the city.

## Culture and life

- World-famous **museums and galleries**, many of them **free**: the British Museum, the National Gallery, the Natural History Museum, Tate Modern.
- **Theatres** in the West End (musicals), Shakespeare's Globe.
- Parks: Hyde Park, Regent's Park – Londoners love green spaces.
- Shopping: Oxford Street, Camden Market, Harrods.
- Transport: the **Underground ("the Tube")** – the oldest metro in the world (1863), red **double-decker buses** and black cabs.
- Problems of a big city: high prices, traffic, crowds of tourists.
