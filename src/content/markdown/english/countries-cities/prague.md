# Prague

Prague (**Praha**) is the **capital city of the Czech Republic** ([[countries-cities/czech-republic]]). People call it "
the city of a hundred spires" or "the heart of Europe". It is one of the most beautiful and most visited cities in the
world.

---

## Basic facts

- Population: about **1.3 million** people (the biggest Czech city).
- It lies on both banks of the **Vltava river**.
- It is the seat of the **president, the government and the parliament**.
- The historical centre is on the **UNESCO World Heritage list**.

## Main sights

- **Prague Castle** – the largest ancient castle complex in the world; the seat of Czech kings in the past and of the
  president today; inside is **St Vitus Cathedral**.
- **Charles Bridge** – a gothic stone bridge from the 14th century, built by king **Charles IV**; it is decorated with
  baroque statues.
- **The Old Town Square** – with the **Astronomical Clock (Orloj)**: every hour, figures of the twelve apostles appear
  in the windows.
- **Wenceslas Square** – the centre of modern Prague, a place of shops and important historical events.
- The **Jewish Quarter (Josefov)** – synagogues and the old Jewish cemetery.
- Other places: Petřín hill with its lookout tower, the National Theatre, the National Museum, Vyšehrad.

## Life in Prague

- Prague has excellent **public transport** – metro (three lines), trams and buses.
- It is the economic centre of the country – many people move there **for work and study** (Charles University is the
  oldest university in Central Europe, founded in 1348).
- Culture: theatres, galleries, concerts, festivals – something for everyone.
- Problems: **expensive housing**, traffic, and **too many tourists** and tourist traps in the centre.

- **capital city** – hlavní město

- **cathedral** – katedrála
- **astronomical clock** – orloj
- **public transport** – městská hromadná doprava
- **crowded** – přeplněný
- **seat of government** – sídlo vlády
