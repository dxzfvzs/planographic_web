# Canada

Canada is the **second largest country in the world** (after Russia), but it has only about **40 million people** – most of them live near the border with the USA. It is famous for its beautiful wild nature, friendly people and high quality of life.

---

## Basic facts

- Canada consists of **ten provinces and three territories**.
- The capital is **Ottawa**; the biggest cities are **Toronto, Montreal and Vancouver**.
- It has **two official languages: English and French** (French is spoken mainly in Quebec).
- Canada is a member of the **Commonwealth** – the head of state is the **British King**, represented by a Governor General; in practice the country is led by the **Prime Minister**.
- Currency: Canadian dollar; the flag shows a red **maple leaf**, the national symbol.

## Geography and nature

- Canada borders **only the USA** ([[countries-cities/usa]]) and three oceans (Atlantic, Pacific, Arctic).
- Huge forests, thousands of **lakes** (the Great Lakes on the US border), the **Rocky Mountains** in the west and polar regions in the north.
- **Niagara Falls** – the famous waterfalls between Canada and the USA.
- Typical animals: **bears, moose, beavers**; winters are long and very cold in most of the country.

## People and culture

- Canada is a **multicultural country** of immigrants from all over the world; it is known for being **open and tolerant**.
- The original inhabitants are the **First Nations and the Inuit**.
- **Ice hockey** is the national sport – many NHL stars are Canadian; other loves: lacrosse, skiing, canoeing.
- Famous Canadian products and symbols: **maple syrup**, lumberjack shirts; famous people: singers Céline Dion, Justin Bieber and Drake, actors Ryan Reynolds and Jim Carrey.
- High quality of life: good healthcare, safety, clean environment – Canadian cities often rank among the **best places to live**.
