# Australia and New Zealand

Australia and New Zealand are two English-speaking countries in the **southern hemisphere** – so their **seasons are opposite to ours**: when we have winter, they have summer and Christmas on the beach. Both are members of the **Commonwealth**, and their head of state is the British King.

---

## Australia

- The **smallest continent and the largest island** in the world; also called "Down Under".
- Population about **26 million** – most people live in cities **on the coast**.
- The capital is **Canberra** (not Sydney!); the biggest cities are **Sydney** (the Opera House, Harbour Bridge) and **Melbourne**.
- The centre of the country is the **outback** – dry, hot desert; the famous red rock **Uluru (Ayers Rock)** is sacred to the **Aboriginal people**, the original inhabitants.
- The **Great Barrier Reef** – the largest coral reef in the world, popular with divers.
- Unique animals: **kangaroos, koalas**, wombats, emus, crocodiles and many dangerous spiders and snakes.
- Australians love **outdoor life**: barbecues, surfing, cricket, rugby; the country exports wool, beef and minerals.
- It began as a **British prison colony** in 1788 – today it is a modern multicultural country.

## New Zealand

- Two main islands (**the North Island and the South Island**) about 2,000 km east of Australia.
- Population only about **5 million** people – and many more **sheep**!
- The capital is **Wellington**; the biggest city is **Auckland**.
- The original inhabitants are the **Maori**; Maori culture (the **haka** war dance, tattoos) is an important part of national identity – the rugby team, the **All Blacks**, performs the haka before matches.
- Beautiful and varied nature: volcanoes, geysers, fjords, mountains and green hills – the **Lord of the Rings** films were made there.
- New Zealanders are called **"Kiwis"** after their national bird, the kiwi, which cannot fly.
- Popular activities: rugby, hiking ("tramping"), extreme sports – **bungee jumping** was invented there.

## Useful vocabulary

- **southern hemisphere** – jižní polokoule
- **coast** – pobřeží
- **desert** – poušť
- **coral reef** – korálový útes
- **original inhabitants / indigenous people** – původní obyvatelé
- **sacred** – posvátný
- **sheep farming** – chov ovcí
- **volcano / geyser** – sopka / gejzír
