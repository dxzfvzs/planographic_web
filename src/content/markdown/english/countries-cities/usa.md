# The USA

The **United States of America** is the third largest country in the world by population (about **335 million** people). It is a superpower with huge influence on world politics, economy and culture – we all know American films, music and brands.

---

## Basic facts

- **50 states** + the capital district; the capital is **Washington, D.C.**, the biggest city is **New York**.
- The flag ("**Stars and Stripes**") has 50 stars (states) and 13 stripes (the original colonies).
- Currency: **US dollar ($)**; national symbol: the bald eagle.
- Independence from Britain was declared on **4 July 1776** (Independence Day).

## Geography

- The country stretches **from the Atlantic to the Pacific Ocean**; neighbours are **Canada** ([[countries-cities/canada]]) and **Mexico**; Alaska and Hawaii are separate states.
- Mountains: the **Rocky Mountains** (west) and the Appalachians (east); the longest river is the **Mississippi–Missouri**.
- Amazing nature in **national parks**: Yellowstone (the first national park in the world), the **Grand Canyon**, Yosemite.
- The climate is very varied – from Alaska's cold to Florida's sunshine; hurricanes and tornados occur.

## Political system

- The USA is a **federal republic**; the head of state is the **president**, elected every four years, who lives in the **White House**.
- The parliament is called **Congress** (the Senate and the House of Representatives).
- The two main parties are the **Democrats and the Republicans**.

## Cities and culture

- **New York** – Manhattan, the Statue of Liberty, Times Square, Central Park, Broadway.
- **Washington, D.C.** – the White House, the Capitol; **Los Angeles** – Hollywood; **San Francisco** – the Golden Gate Bridge; Chicago, Las Vegas, Miami.
- The USA is a **nation of immigrants** – a mix of cultures from the whole world.
- Culture: Hollywood films, jazz, rock, hip hop and country music, famous universities (Harvard, MIT), technology companies (Apple, Google).
- Sports: American football (the Super Bowl), basketball (NBA), baseball, ice hockey (NHL).
- Holidays: **Thanksgiving** (turkey dinner in November), Independence Day, Halloween ([[culture-leisure/holidays-festivals]]).
