# Education, Our School

Education prepares us for our future life and career. In this topic I should describe the **Czech school system**, compare it with **Britain or the USA**, and talk about **my own school**.

---

## The Czech school system

- **Nursery school (kindergarten)** – ages 3–6; the last year is compulsory.
- **Primary school (základní škola)** – ages 6–15, nine years; school attendance is **compulsory**.
- **Secondary school** – usually four years, finished with the **maturita** (school-leaving exam):
    - **grammar school (gymnázium)** – general education, prepares for university,
    - **specialised secondary schools** – technical, business, art schools (like ours),
    - **vocational schools** – training for a trade (apprenticeship).
- **University** – bachelor's (3 years), master's (2 more years), doctoral degrees.
- State schools are **free**; students get marks from 1 (excellent) to 5 (fail).

## Our school

- I study at the **Secondary School of Graphics in Brno** (Střední škola grafická).
- My study field is focused on **graphic design and printing (polygraphy)** – we learn typography, graphic software, photography and printing technologies.
- Besides specialised subjects we also have general ones: Czech, English, maths.
- The school has **computer classrooms with professional software** and workshops with printing machines, so we get **practical training**, not only theory.
- School year: September to June, two terms; lessons usually start at 8 a.m. and last 45 minutes.
- At the end of the fourth year we take the **maturita exam** – Czech, English (or maths) and our specialised subjects.

## Education in the UK and the USA

- **UK:** primary school, then secondary school; at 16 students take **GCSE exams**, at 18 **A-levels**, which decide university entrance. Some families pay for **private ("public") schools** with uniforms and strict rules.
- **USA:** elementary, middle and **high school**; students finish with a **graduation ceremony** (caps and gowns). Universities are called **colleges** and are usually very expensive – students take loans or try to get **scholarships**.
- Differences from Czech schools: uniforms in the UK, more choice of subjects, different marking (letters A–F).

## Useful vocabulary

- **compulsory school attendance** – povinná školní docházka
- **school-leaving exam** – maturitní zkouška
- **vocational school / apprenticeship** – učiliště / vyučení
- **study field** – studijní obor
- **timetable** – rozvrh
- **term** – pololetí
- **scholarship** – stipendium
- **degree** – akademický titul
