# Jobs

Work is a big part of adult life – we spend about a third of the day at work. In this topic I talk about **choosing a career, looking for a job** and **my own plans for the future**.

---

## Choosing a job

- When choosing a job, people consider: **salary**, job satisfaction, **working hours**, distance from home, career growth and **job security**.
- Jobs can be: **manual** (builder, mechanic) × **mental** (teacher, programmer); **indoor × outdoor**; employees × **self-employed** people (freelancers, entrepreneurs).
- Some professions are **well-paid** (doctors, IT specialists, managers), others are badly paid but important (shop assistants, carers).
- Today it is normal to **change jobs** several times during life; new jobs appear (social media manager, AI specialist) and old ones disappear.

## Looking for a job

1. I look for offers on **job websites**, social networks (LinkedIn) or through friends.
2. I send a **CV (curriculum vitae)** – education, work experience, skills, languages – and a **covering letter** (why I want the job and why they should choose me).
3. If they like it, they invite me to a **job interview**: I should come on time, dress well, and be ready for questions about my strengths, weaknesses and experience.
4. Then I sign a **contract** – it states my salary, working hours, holidays and the **notice period**.
- Many students get their first experience through **part-time jobs (brigády)** or **internships** during their studies.

## My future career

- I study **graphic design and printing** ([[education-daily-life/education-our-school]]), so I would like to work as a **graphic designer** – in a design studio, an advertising agency or a printing house.
- Another option is to work as a **freelancer** – the advantage is freedom, the disadvantage is that income is not certain and I have to find my own clients.
- I may also **continue studying** at university first.
- My dream job should be **creative**, it should pay enough to live on, and it should not be boring – I don't want to hate Monday mornings.

## Useful vocabulary

- **salary / wages** – plat / mzda
- **working hours** – pracovní doba
- **employer × employee** – zaměstnavatel × zaměstnanec
- **self-employed / freelancer** – OSVČ / na volné noze
- **CV and covering letter** – životopis a motivační dopis
- **job interview** – pracovní pohovor
- **to be promoted** – být povýšen
- **to be unemployed / to be fired** – být nezaměstnaný / dostat výpověď

## Questions to practise

- What is important for you when choosing a job – money, or enjoying the work?
- What would you like to do after finishing school?
- What does a person need to succeed at a job interview?
- What are the advantages and disadvantages of being a freelancer?
- Have you ever had a part-time job? What did you learn from it?
