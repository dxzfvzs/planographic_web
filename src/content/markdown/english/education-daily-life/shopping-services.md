# Shopping and Services

We all go shopping – some people love it, some hate it. In this topic I talk about **types of shops, shopping habits, online shopping** and the **services** we use in everyday life.

---

## Types of shops

- **Supermarket / hypermarket** – food and almost everything else under one roof (Tesco, Albert, Lidl).
- **Shopping centre (mall)** – many shops, cafés and a cinema in one building; popular for spending a whole afternoon.
- **Small specialised shops**: baker's, butcher's, greengrocer's, chemist's (drugstore), newsagent's, bookshop, florist's.
- **Markets** – fresh local food, farmers' markets; **second-hand shops** – cheap and ecological.
- Shops in the centre × big stores on the edge of town (retail parks).

## How we pay and shop

- We pay **in cash** or **by card** – today often **contactless**, by phone or watch.
- Shops attract customers with **sales, discounts and loyalty cards**; Black Friday.
- Be careful about **impulse buying** – buying things we don't really need.
- If a product is faulty, I can **return it or complain** (make a complaint) – usually within two years; I need the **receipt**.

## Online shopping

- **Advantages:** open 24/7, lower prices, easy comparison, huge choice, delivery to your home or a pick-up point.
- **Disadvantages:** you can't try the goods, you wait for delivery, returning things is annoying, risk of **fake e-shops**; too easy to spend money.
- People often go to a shop to **try things on** and then buy them cheaper online.

## Services

- **Post office** – sending letters and parcels; **bank** – accounts, loans, exchange office for currency.
- **Hairdresser's, barber's, beauty salon**; **dry cleaner's and laundry**.
- **Repair services** – shoe repair, car service (garage), phone repair.
- **Restaurants, cafés, food delivery** ([[culture-leisure/food-meals]]), taxi and ride apps, libraries, fitness centres ([[culture-leisure/healthy-lifestyle]]).

## Useful vocabulary

- **to go shopping / to do the shopping** – jít nakupovat / obstarat nákup
- **customer** – zákazník
- **discount / sale** – sleva / výprodej
- **receipt** – účtenka
- **to make a complaint** – reklamovat
- **to try on** – vyzkoušet si (oblečení)
- **pick-up point** – výdejní místo
- **queue** – fronta

## Questions to practise

- Do you enjoy shopping? What do you usually buy?
- Where does your family do the weekly food shopping?
- Do you prefer shopping online or in real shops? Why?
- What can you do if the product you bought stops working?
- What services do you and your family use regularly?
