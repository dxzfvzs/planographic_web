# Housing and Living

Everyone needs a place to live. In this topic I describe **types of housing, my own home**, and compare **living in a city and in the countryside**. I should also be able to describe rooms and furniture.

---

## Types of housing

- **Flat (apartment)** – in a **block of flats**; in the Czech Republic many people live in concrete blocks called *paneláky* from the communist era.
- **Family house** – with a garden, typical in villages and suburbs; **terraced houses** are joined in a row (typical in the UK).
- **Cottage / country house** – many Czech families have a **weekend cottage** (chata, chalupa) – a very Czech tradition.
- Other: studio flat (one room), dormitory for students, bungalow, farm.
- **Renting × owning**: young people usually **rent a flat** (pay monthly rent to a landlord); buying your own home is very expensive today – prices and **mortgages** are a big topic.

## My home

- I live with my family **in a flat / in a house** in Brno / near Brno.
- We have a kitchen, a living room, bedrooms, a bathroom and a toilet, a hall; (a balcony / a garden / a cellar).
- **My room** is my kingdom: there is a bed, a desk with my computer (important for graphic design work!), a wardrobe, shelves with books and posters on the walls.
- In the **living room** there is a sofa, armchairs, a TV and a coffee table; the **kitchen** has a cooker, an oven, a fridge, a dishwasher and a washing machine.
- Household appliances make life easier; smart home technology is becoming common.

## City × countryside

- **Living in a city:** everything is close – schools, shops, doctors, culture, public transport; but also noise, traffic, pollution, higher prices and anonymous neighbours.
- **Living in the countryside:** fresh air, nature, space, garden, quiet, community life; but you must **commute** to work or school, and there are fewer services and less entertainment.
- Many families move to the **suburbs** – a compromise between both.
- My opinion: as a student I prefer the city; maybe later I would like a house with a garden.

## Useful vocabulary

- **block of flats** – panelák / bytový dům
- **terraced house** – řadový dům
- **suburb** – předměstí
- **to rent / rent** – pronajímat si / nájem
- **landlord** – majitel bytu (pronajímatel)
- **mortgage** – hypotéka
- **furniture** – nábytek
- **household appliances** – domácí spotřebiče
