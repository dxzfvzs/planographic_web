# Holidays and Festivals

Holidays and festivals bring families and friends together and keep **traditions** alive. In this topic I compare **Czech celebrations** with holidays in **English-speaking countries**.

---

## Czech holidays and traditions

- **Christmas (Vánoce)** – the most important family holiday:
    - the main evening is **Christmas Eve, 24 December**: traditional dinner of **fried carp and potato salad**, then presents under the **Christmas tree**, brought by **Ježíšek (baby Jesus)**,
    - traditions: baking Christmas sweets, advent calendars, carols, mistletoe, cutting an apple, floating walnut boats.
- **Easter (Velikonoce)** – spring holiday: boys whip girls gently with a **braided willow whip (pomlázka)** and get **painted eggs**; Easter Monday is a public holiday.
- **Witches' Night (30 April)** – bonfires and "burning witches"; **1 May** – lovers kiss under a blooming tree.
- **St Nicholas Day (5 December)** – St Nicholas with an angel and a devil brings sweets to good children.
- State holidays: 28 October (founding of Czechoslovakia, 1918), 17 November (Velvet Revolution, 1989), 5–6 July (Cyril and Methodius, Jan Hus).
- Name days – a Czech speciality: every day in the calendar has a name.

## Holidays in the UK and the USA

- **Christmas** – presents come on the **morning of 25 December**, brought by **Santa Claus / Father Christmas** through the chimney into **stockings**; typical dinner: roast **turkey**, Christmas pudding; 26 December is **Boxing Day** (sales!).
- **Halloween (31 October)** – children in costumes go **trick-or-treating**; pumpkins (jack-o'-lanterns), scary decorations; originally a Celtic festival.
- **Thanksgiving (USA, 4th Thursday of November)** – family dinner with turkey; remembering the first settlers (Pilgrims); the next day is Black Friday.
- **Independence Day (USA, 4 July)** – fireworks, barbecues, parades ([[countries-cities/usa]]).
- **Guy Fawkes Night / Bonfire Night (UK, 5 November)** – fireworks and bonfires,
- **St Patrick's Day (17 March)** – Irish holiday: green colour, parades,
- **Valentine's Day (14 February)** – cards and gifts for loved ones – now common in Czechia too.

## Useful vocabulary

- **public holiday** – státní svátek
- **to celebrate / celebration** – slavit / oslava
- **tradition / custom** – tradice / zvyk
- **Christmas Eve** – Štědrý večer
- **carol** – koleda
- **fireworks** – ohňostroj
- **costume** – kostým, maska
- **trick or treat** – „koledu, nebo vám něco provedu" (Halloween)
