# Food and Meals

Food is a daily necessity, but also a pleasure and part of every culture. In this topic I describe **meals of the day, Czech cuisine, British and American food** and **eating out**.

---

## Meals of the day

- **Breakfast** – bread or rolls with butter and cheese/ham, cereal, yoghurt; tea or coffee. (English breakfast is famous: eggs, bacon, sausages, beans – but most Brits eat it only at weekends.)
- **Snack (mid-morning)** – fruit or a sandwich at school.
- **Lunch** – in Czechia the **main hot meal of the day**: soup + main course; students eat in the school canteen. In Britain lunch is often just a sandwich, and the main meal is **dinner** in the evening.
- **Dinner / supper** – Czech dinners are often cold (bread, cheese, vegetables) or light hot meals.

## Czech cuisine

- Traditional Czech food is **tasty but heavy**: meat, sauces and **dumplings (knedlíky)**.
- National dishes: **roast pork with dumplings and sauerkraut** (vepřo-knedlo-zelo), **svíčková** (beef in cream sauce with dumplings and cranberries), goulash, schnitzel (řízek) with potato salad, fried cheese (smažák).
- Sweet dishes: fruit dumplings, pancakes (palačinky), koláče, Christmas sweets ([[culture-leisure/holidays-festivals]]).
- Czech **beer** is world-famous ([[countries-cities/czech-republic]]).

## British and American food

- **UK:** fish and chips, **Sunday roast** with Yorkshire pudding, shepherd's pie, sandwiches, **afternoon tea** with scones; curry is extremely popular thanks to Indian restaurants.
- **USA:** hamburgers, hot dogs, steaks, mac and cheese, pancakes with maple syrup, donuts; **fast food** culture was born there (McDonald's, KFC), and portions are famously huge.
- Both countries are truly multicultural in food – Italian, Chinese, Mexican, Japanese restaurants everywhere.

## Eating out and cooking

- **Eating out**: restaurants, pizzerias, bistros, fast food, food courts in shopping centres; **food delivery** apps are very popular.
- In a restaurant: menu, ordering, asking for the bill, **tip** (about 10 %).
- **Cooking at home** is cheaper and healthier; cooking shows and recipes on social media inspire people; now it's your turn to freestyle — name a dish you can cook, e.g. scrambled eggs, pasta, or a simple soup.
- Modern trends: vegetarian and vegan food, bio/organic products, local food, **food waste** reduction ([[family-global/global-problems]]).

## Useful vocabulary

- **main course / side dish** – hlavní chod / příloha
- **dumplings** – knedlíky
- **sauerkraut** – kysané zelí
- **roast / fried / boiled** – pečený / smažený / vařený
- **to order / to book a table** – objednat si / rezervovat stůl
- **the bill / a tip** – účet / spropitné
- **takeaway / food delivery** – jídlo s sebou / rozvoz jídla
- **taste / tasty** – chuť / chutný
