# Literature

Books entertain us, teach us and develop our imagination. In this topic I talk about **why people read, literary genres, famous writers** – and mainly about **a book I have read**, because that is what examiners usually ask about.

---

## Why do people read?

- for **entertainment and relaxation** – a good story lets us escape everyday life,
- to **learn something** – non-fiction, biographies, textbooks,
- reading improves **vocabulary, imagination and concentration**,
- today books compete with films, series and social media – people read less, but audiobooks and **e-books** are popular; printed books survive because we love their feel (as a graphic school student I appreciate **book design** too).

## Literary genres

- **Fiction:** novel, short story, fairy tale, poetry, drama (plays),
- popular genres: **crime and detective stories, thrillers, fantasy, science fiction, horror, romance, historical novels, comics**,
- **Non-fiction:** biographies, travel books, popular science, cookbooks.

## Famous writers

- **British:** William **Shakespeare** (Romeo and Juliet, Hamlet), Jane Austen, Charles Dickens, Agatha Christie (queen of crime), J. R. R. Tolkien (The Lord of the Rings), George **Orwell** (1984, Animal Farm), J. K. **Rowling** (Harry Potter).
- **American:** Ernest Hemingway, Mark Twain (Tom Sawyer), Stephen King (horror), John Steinbeck (Of Mice and Men).
- **Czech:** Karel Čapek (he gave the world the word **"robot"**), Franz Kafka (wrote in German in Prague), Milan Kundera, Bohumil Hrabal.

## How to talk about a book

Structure for "my favourite book" (choose one you really know!):

1. **Basic facts** – title, author, genre.
2. **Setting** – where and when the story takes place.
3. **Main characters** – who they are, what they are like.
4. **Plot** – what happens, in a few sentences (don't retell every detail; use present tense: "The story follows…").
5. **My opinion** – why I liked it, what it made me think about, who I would recommend it to.

*Tip: the Czech literature list for maturita can help – e.g. 1984, Of Mice and Men or The Metamorphosis work in English too.*

## Useful vocabulary

- **fiction × non-fiction** – beletrie × literatura faktu
- **novel / short story** – román / povídka
- **plot** – děj, zápletka
- **main character / hero** – hlavní postava / hrdina
- **setting** – prostředí (místo a čas děje)
- **to take place** – odehrávat se
- **to recommend** – doporučit
- **audiobook / e-book** – audiokniha / e-kniha

## Questions to practise

- Do you like reading? How often do you read and what?
- What genres are popular among young people?
- Tell me about a book you have read – what was it about?
- Do you prefer printed books or e-books? Why?
- Is it better to read the book or watch the film adaptation?
