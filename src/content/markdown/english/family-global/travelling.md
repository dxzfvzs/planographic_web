# Travelling

Travelling broadens the mind – we see new places, meet new people and try new food. In this topic I talk about **why and how people travel, types of holidays** and **my own travel experience and dreams**.

---

## Why do people travel?

- for **holidays and relaxation** – to escape everyday routine,
- to **explore**: foreign cultures, history, food, languages (best way to practise English!),
- for **work or study** (business trips, study exchanges like Erasmus),
- to visit **family and friends**.

## Means of transport

- **By plane** – the fastest for long distances; cheap airlines exist, but: airport security, luggage limits, delays, and it's bad for the environment.
- **By train** – comfortable, you can walk around, ecological; in Europe very practical.
- **By car** – freedom and flexibility, door to door; but traffic jams and parking.
- **By bus/coach** – cheap (FlixBus etc.), but long and less comfortable.
- Others: ferry, ship (cruises), bike, **hitchhiking**, walking (hiking trips).

## Types of holidays

- **Seaside holiday** – Czechs love Croatia and Italy: swimming, sunbathing, ice cream.
- **City break** – a weekend in London, Vienna, Paris: sightseeing, galleries, cafés ([[countries-cities/london]]).
- **Active holiday** – hiking in the mountains, cycling, skiing (Alps), water sports.
- **Package holiday** – a travel agency arranges everything (flight, hotel, meals); opposite: **travelling independently** – cheaper and more adventurous.
- **Staycation** – holiday at home or in your own country; Czech favourites: cottages, camping, castles.
- **Accommodation**: hotel, guesthouse (B&B), hostel (cheap, for young travellers), camping, Airbnb, staying with friends.

## Preparing for a trip abroad

- documents: **passport or ID card** (in the EU), visa for some countries, **travel insurance**,
- booking flights and accommodation online, checking reviews,
- **packing**: clothes for the weather, chargers, medicine, adapters,
- exchanging money or paying by card; learning a few phrases of the local language,
- at the airport: check-in, security check, boarding pass, gate, boarding.

## My travel experience and dreams

- Be ready to describe **one real trip**: where, when, who with, how you travelled, what you saw, what you enjoyed, one funny or bad moment.
- My dream destination: … (e.g. Japan, New Zealand ([[countries-cities/australia-new-zealand]]), the USA) – and **why**.

## Useful vocabulary

- **to go sightseeing** – prohlížet si památky
- **package holiday** – zájezd s cestovkou
- **accommodation** – ubytování
- **travel insurance** – cestovní pojištění
- **luggage / suitcase / backpack** – zavazadla / kufr / batoh
- **delay / to miss a flight** – zpoždění / zmeškat let
- **abroad** – v zahraničí
- **destination** – cíl cesty

## Questions to practise

- Do you like travelling? Where have you been?
- What means of transport do you prefer and why?
- Describe your last holiday – where did you go and what did you do there?
- Do you prefer holidays by the sea or in the mountains?
- If you could travel anywhere in the world, where would you go and why?
