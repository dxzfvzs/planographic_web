# Family and Relationships

Family is usually the most important group in our life – the people who **support us**. In this topic I describe **my family**, talk about **types of families** and about **relationships** between people.

---

## Family members

- **Immediate (nuclear) family:** mother, father, siblings (brother, sister), children.
- **Extended family:** grandparents, aunts and uncles, cousins, nephews and nieces.
- After marriage: in-laws – mother-in-law, brother-in-law…; **step-family**: stepfather, stepmother, stepbrother (after remarriage).
- Be ready to describe: names, ages, jobs, what they look like and **what they are like** (character), what we do together.

## Types of families today

- **Nuclear family** (parents + children) is the traditional model,
- **single-parent families** – after divorce (about half of Czech marriages end in divorce) or separation,
- **patchwork families** – new partners bring children from previous relationships,
- couples living together **without marriage** are common today; people marry later and have fewer children,
- in the past, **several generations** lived together; today grandparents usually live separately, but they often help with childcare.

## Relationships

- **Friendship** – a good friend is someone I can **trust and rely on**; friends support each other, share hobbies and secrets; "A friend in need is a friend indeed."
- **Partnership and love** – dating (today often through apps), first love, engagement (getting engaged), **wedding**, living together.
- **Generation gap** – parents and teenagers sometimes don't understand each other: different music, technology, opinions and rules (curfews, chores, screen time). The key is **communication and compromise**.
- Family celebrations: birthdays, name days, Christmas, weddings, anniversaries ([[culture-leisure/holidays-festivals]]).

## My family and me

- Describe your family honestly but simply: who you live with, who you get on with best, family habits (Sunday lunches, holidays together, game nights).
- Roles at home: who cooks, who does the shopping, what chores I do ([[education-daily-life/everyday-life]]).
- Future: Would I like to get married one day? Have children? Live near my parents?

## Useful vocabulary

- **siblings** – sourozenci
- **cousin** – bratranec / sestřenice
- **to get on well with somebody** – dobře s někým vycházet
- **to rely on somebody** – spolehnout se na někoho
- **divorce / divorced** – rozvod / rozvedený
- **single-parent family** – neúplná rodina
- **generation gap** – generační propast
- **to get engaged / married** – zasnoubit se / vzít se

## Questions to practise

- Tell me about your family. Who do you live with?
- Who are you closest to in your family and why?
- What makes a good friend?
- Why do so many marriages end in divorce today?
- What arguments do teenagers and parents usually have?
